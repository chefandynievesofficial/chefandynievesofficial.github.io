Bio

Started the culinary industry about 10 years ago, since then I have got the opportunity to diverse into all different kinds of food cultures. It all started when as a kid my grandmother would prepared all sorts of dishes for the family. As of that, I began to feel inspired and begin to fall in love with food.

In 2010, I started “La Escuela Hotelera” which is Culinary Arts School. Here is where my real passion for food was discovered and proudly graduated with top grades for cooking skills. Few months later, I got hired as a banquet chef at one of the most well-known touristic hotel called “Concha” in Condado, Puerto Rico.

Pursuing my desire for learning culture dishes, I began to work in “Ikebana” a sushi restaurant consider famous for its authentic Japanese flavor. Here, is where I learned from the most basic recipe to the most laborious sushi piece. Moving forward with my career I step into my fist opportunity “Olive Mediterranean” a well recognize fine dining restaurant, where I dedicated over one year to the Mediterranean cuisine.

By the midst of 2012 my biggest food industry door opened up at “Dorado Beach a Ritz – Carlton Reserve” in Puerto Rico. Given me the opportunity to work in “Mi Casa” a fine dining restaurant run by Jose Andres, a Spanish well-credited Chef. “Mi Casa” was placed #4 from 101 best restaurants in all Latin America. The reserve was awarded for being a AAA Five Diamond hotel, as well named the Caribbean’s 2017 Forbes Five-Star hotel and spa.

After mastering the Spanish cuisine for over three years at “Mi Casa” in “Dorado Beach a Ritz – Carlton Reserve” my brilliant cooking skills took me to JW Marriott in Los Angeles Live. Where I had the unique opportunity to learned from Jacob Ramos an executive Chef. Our professional culinary team was in charge of all kinds of VIP events, including gathering celebrities, famous basketball teams, (“Lakers”), banquets among other important occasions.
